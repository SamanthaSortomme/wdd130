Good products need to be put on in a specific order. They have a molecular weight (I took a class that told me to say things like that so you'll think I'm really smart and buy more products from me) and products with smaller molecules go on first so they are not sitting on top of products with bigger molecules. If you don't know which order your products go on, a good rule is the thinner ones first then the thicker ones. On that note….
Please don't buy moisturizer. It does nothing for your skin. It sits on top like some fabled princess waving a wand made of unicorn tears and sapphires found in Atlantis. I've heard the myth that it's like a windshield, keeping the good products on your skin and the bad pollutants in the air off of your face. The problem with that is that your products are not able to pack up and book flights off of your skin and everything in the air is still going to stick to your face. If you love that analogy and it makes you comfortable then think of your sunscreen as the new and improved windshield. The best defense for your skin is going to be making it healthy. If products soak into your skin really fast your barrier is compromised and you need to get it fixed with good homecare.

I love giving facials, I give them to my friends and family for free. They are relaxing and enjoyable. As a professional I have access to products that are stronger than what you can safely get (I'm looking at you Amazon who sold my client shampoo someone had filled with Nair and all of her hair fell out in clumps *true story) but having stronger products isn't the end all be all of skincare. I like to relate it to your teeth. A good cleaning from the dentist doesn't do much good if you don't brush your teeth for the 6 months between. You can get the same results with homecare that you can with treatments; it just takes more time. A lot of people look at skin care as something they want to do until their current issue goes away. The problem with this mentality is often the issue will come back. There are people who only touch their face when they feel they need to, like a breakout. It works for some but it's usually easier to keep your skin healthy all of the time than to try to get it healthy enough to fix whenever you dislike what you're seeing in the mirror.
